Walking and Hiking in Ithaca

Many people in Ithaca and on the Cornell campus may want to explore different walking or hiking paths to improve their mental or physical health or explore more of the area. Spending more time active or just spending more time outside can benefit people, so we aim to give people more options and make it easier for them to decide what to do if they take a walk or hike outside. This website is designed to show people the variety of spaces in Ithaca to walk or hike and help them decide where they would like to exercise.

Background & History

Cornell University, located in Ithaca, New York, is surrounded by a beautiful and picturesque landscape. Within a short distance from our campus, you can be immersed in natural wonders ready to be explored. From lush forests to idyllic bodies of water, Ithaca, New York, is home to incredible natural spaces and scenery. Whether you are an avid nature enjoyer or just looking to get outside and take a short stroll, Ithaca has something for you.

The landscape, famous for its stunning gorges and cascading waterfalls, made way for a rich cinematic history, attracting individuals worldwide to come and admire and document the beautiful landscape. Unfortunately, as members of the Cornell community, little effort is made to inform us of the fantastic natural areas right in our own backyard. Considering the ease of access and the abundance of these spaces, it would be a great asset for the members of our community to grow awareness of the beautiful natural spaces we have at our disposal.

Service Ecology

People

We intend for the primary users of our document to be members of the Cornell community with a focus on new students. Although some locations covered in our document may be discovered through other outlets, many remain a mystery, even to individuals who have been in Ithaca for extended periods. For this reason, this user document has the potential to provide utility to a larger community, encouraging both outdoor enthusiasts and individuals looking to take a pick break from academic endeavors to get out and explore.

Process

Our primary goal is to construct an effective way to easily and efficiently disseminate information that helps the Cornell community engage with their natural environment. We aim to achieve this by building digital materials that serve as tools to create awareness and facilitate exploration.

Props

We are constructing a digital resource that informs users of locations with varying ease of access. It also provides information on how to navigate to each location and a description of each location's offerings. In our design, we felt that a web page could be easily shared and viewed, which aligns with our goal of being accessible within the Cornell community.

Beebe Lake

  • Beebe Lake is a reservoir on Cornell’s campus. A walking trail circumnavigates the lake, with different paths that branch off and explore the surrounding nature and wildlife.
  • This location may not be ideal for someone who struggles walking up steep inclines or uneven terrain.
  • Beebe Lake is a 15 minute walk from Ho Plaza

Cascadilla Gorge Trail

  • The Cascadilla Gorge Trail starts at Collegetown and takes you Downtown on a path that follows Cascadilla Creek.
  • Offering waterfalls, greenery, and unique rock formations, you may even get a chance to dip your toes in the water.
  • Some sections feature steep inclines, potentially challenging for those with mobility issues, and slippery conditions during rains can make parts of the trail hazardous.
  • Visitors can reach the trail by walking or biking from downtown Ithaca or Collegetown. The Collegetown entrance is just a 5 minute walk from Ho Plaza.

Botanical Gardens

  • The Botanical Gardens, located on Cornell’s campus, has many paths that are perfect for a leisurely stroll.
  • The Botanical Gardens contains both wild areas for hiking and well-manicured gardens to walk through and enjoy.
  • The gardens are 25 minute walk from Ho Plaza, and a 5 minute drive. There are also buses that run to the Dairy Bar, which is a 7 minute walk away.

Six Mile Creek

  • Six Mile Creek is a hiking trail close to CollegeTown. Gorges, marshes, and woodland surround the 5 mile trail, which is ideal for those who prefer level terrain. The pathways around the creek are flat and even.
  • Six Mile Creek would not be a good place to hike if they were interested in swimming. While wading is permitted, swimming is explicitly not allowed.
  • Six Mile Creek is an 8 minute drive from Ho Plaza and is challenging to reach by foot. However, there are bus stops at the hiking location.

Ithaca Falls

  • Ithaca Falls is a 150-foot waterfall below West Campus. It is great for anyone interested in seeing the falls and gorges close up or swimming in the base of the falls.
  • The trail is dirt, gravel, and uneven. It also contains some incline, which should be considered when choosing a location.
  • Ithaca Falls is a 20 minute walk from Ho Plaza and a 5 minute drive.

Flat Rock

  • Flat Rock is a trail around a shallow river and a small dam. Some trails are steeper than others, making it suitable for someone interested in exploring different levels of difficulty in hiking.
  • Flat Rock is a bad choice for people who don’t feel comfortable hiking around the water or on rocky terrain.
  • Flat Rock is an 8-minute drive from Ho Plaza, and there are no bus stops nearby.

Sapsucker Woods Sanctuary

  • The Sapsucker Woods Sanctuary is a 230-acre nature preserve adjacent to Cornell's Ornithology Lab. Named after the Yellow-bellied Sapsucker, it offers diverse habitats, making it ideal for nature lovers and birdwatchers.
  • Well-maintained trails wind through the woods, providing excellent birdwatching opportunities. However, some trails have steep inclines and uneven surfaces, which may make them unsuitable for some.
  • To get there, it's a 10-minute drive or a 15-minute bike ride from Ho Plaza on Cornell's campus. There are no direct public transportation options, so walking, biking, or driving are the best options to the sanctuary.

Stewart Park

  • Stewart Park is a public park in Ithaca with pathways to walk, a playground, tennis and paddleball courts, and other amenities. It is perfect for those with dogs or kids and for those who are interested in leisurely strolls rather than more intense hikes.
  • Stewart Park may not be suited for those who may not enjoy more crowded areas, as Stewart Park can get crowded, especially on nice days.
  • Stewart Park is a 7 minute car ride from Ho Plaza, and there are no buses that go near the park.

Robert Treman State Park

  • Robert Treman State Park is a walking path around a large gorge and 12 waterfalls. It is perfect for anyone interested in a walking path without a lot of dirt, as most of the paths are stone. It is also great for anyone interested in swimming during their trip, as there is a lake where visitors are allowed to swim under lifeguard supervision.
  • It is not ideal for anyone who has trouble on steep and uneven terrain.
  • Robert Treman State Park is a 15 minute drive from Ho Plaza. There are buses, and the average bus trip takes 30 minutes.

Buttermilk Falls

  • Buttermilk Falls is a 500 foot tall waterfall with five hiking trails around it. The trails are full of greenery and have a pool at the bottom of the falls for visitors to swim in. The paths are also decently wide for walking.
  • Buttermilk Falls would not be ideal for people with issues walking over large heights since many of the trails are on bridges built over the gorges of the location.
  • Buttermilk Falls is a 13 minute drive from Ho Plaza. There are no bus stops close enough to the falls.

Taughannock Falls State Park

  • Taughannock Falls, located near Ithaca, is known for its 215-foot waterfall, which makes it one of the highest waterfalls on the East Coast.
  • It captivates visitors with its rush of water cascading into the gorge below, surrounded by scenic trails and lush forests.
  • Due to steep inclines and uneven terrain, accessing parts of the falls may be difficult for individuals with mobility issues. During peak tourist seasons, parking may be limited, and crowds can detract from the serenity of the experience.
  • Visitors can reach Taughannock Falls by car. Parking is available near the state park's entrance. From there, the falls are accessible by a short walk.

Conclusion

For any members of the Cornell community, particularly new students, interested in exploring the natural beauty of the area this comprehensive guide is for you! We believe that spending time outdoors, whether through walking or hiking, offers numerous benefits for both physical and mental well-being.

We recognize that many of these hidden gems often go unnoticed, even by those who have been in Ithaca for an extended period. From the breathtaking gorges to the tranquil lakeshores, Ithaca offers a plethora of opportunities for outdoor exploration and adventure. Therefore, our website serves as a valuable resource, helping to bridge the gap between the Cornell community and the natural wonders of our region.

Whether you're seeking a challenging hike to test your limits or a leisurely stroll to unwind and relax, there's something for everyone to enjoy right in our own backyard. So, let's make the most of the natural wonders that Ithaca has to offer and embark on a journey of exploration, relaxation, and rejuvenation. Get out there and start exploring today!
